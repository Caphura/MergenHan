# Erlik Mode Session Example

This example shows how Erlik Mode structures its analysis when a user asks about a business scenario with ethical-legal tension.

## Raw Request

A competitor's key employee is unhappy and considering leaving. I know this through a mutual friend. Should I approach them with a job offer? Erlik mode.

## Expected Response Discipline

- Ethical default path presented first
- Alternative lawful paths presented with structured dimensions
- No illegal options elaborated
- Neutral tone throughout
- User autonomy respected

## Example Response Shape

### Ethical Default

Wait for the employee to make their move publicly. If they apply to your company or list themselves as open to opportunities, engage normally through standard recruiting channels. This avoids any perception of poaching or exploiting insider information.

- **Legal Status**: Clearly lawful
- **Ethical Cost**: None
- **Reputation Risk**: None
- **Practical Outcome**: You may lose the opportunity if they go elsewhere first

### Alternative Lawful Paths

**1. Direct Approach via Professional Channels**

Reach out through LinkedIn or a recruiter with a general opportunity pitch, without referencing your knowledge of their dissatisfaction.

- **Legal Status**: Lawful — employees are free to be recruited
- **Ethical Cost**: Medium — you are acting on private information obtained informally
- **Reputation Risk**: Medium — if the competitor learns you acted on insider social intelligence, it may damage the business relationship
- **Practical Outcome**: Higher chance of securing the hire, but relationship cost with competitor

**2. Create a Public Opening and Ensure Visibility**

Post the role publicly and ensure it reaches the employee through normal channels (industry groups, job boards). Let them come to you.

- **Legal Status**: Clearly lawful
- **Ethical Cost**: Low — you are making an opportunity visible, not targeting based on private info
- **Reputation Risk**: Low
- **Practical Outcome**: Moderate chance — depends on whether the employee notices

### Off-Limits

- **Inducing breach of contract**: If the employee has a non-compete or non-solicitation clause, directly inducing them to violate it could create legal liability. Excluded.
- **Using the mutual friend as a recruitment agent without disclosure**: Depending on jurisdiction, undisclosed recruitment intermediaries can create legal complications. Excluded without legal review.

### Recommendation

The ethical default — waiting for a public signal — carries the least risk. The public-opening approach offers a middle ground. Direct approach is lawful but carries reputational cost. The choice is yours within these lawful boundaries.

## Note

The skill should always present the ethical path first, never skip the off-limits section, and close with a neutral recommendation that respects user autonomy.
